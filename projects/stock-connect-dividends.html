Stock Connect & Cash Dividend Policy

Independent Researcher · Jan 2024 – Present · Advisor: Prof. Dayuan Li (SHU)

Investigates how the liberalisation of China’s equity market via the Shanghai-Hong Kong and Shenzhen-Hong Kong Stock Connect programs reshapes listed firms’ cash dividend strategies.

Abstract

China’s equity market long prioritised financing over investor returns, generating volatility and limited shareholder protection. To remedy structural imbalances, regulators progressively opened the market through the Stock Connect initiatives that link Mainland exchanges with Hong Kong, aiming to introduce global governance standards and investor discipline.

Treating the phased rollout of Shanghai-Hong Kong and Shenzhen-Hong Kong Stock Connect as a quasi-natural experiment, this study analyses A-share firms from 2007–2019 using a Difference-in-Differences approach. The policy significantly lifts cash dividend payouts, operating through suppressed earnings management, lower financing constraints, and improved investment efficiency. Heterogeneity tests reveal stronger effects for state-owned, smaller-scale, highly competitive, and high-disclosure firms, while spillover analysis confirms dividend-driven gains in firm value.

Empirical Highlights

  • Constructs treatment and control cohorts aligned with the staged opening of Stock Connect to capture causal dividend effects.
  • Demonstrates that improved investor access curbs opportunistic earnings management and relaxes financing constraints.
  • Links disciplined dividend policy to higher firm valuation, documenting a positive spillover from market liberalisation.

Methodology & Data

  • Difference-in-Differences
  • A-share panel dataset (2007–2019)
  • Propensity score matching
  • Robustness checks
  • Policy evaluation
