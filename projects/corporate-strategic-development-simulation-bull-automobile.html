Corporate Strategic Development Simulation – Bull Automobile

Key Member & Data Analyst · Mar 2025 – Jun 2025 · Cesim Global Challenge (10 rounds, 11 firms)

Led a 10-round corporate strategy simulation and built a full analytics pipeline in Python to uncover what drives cumulative shareholder return (CTSR). Using statistical tests and machine learning, I quantified the impact of long-term strategic choices (R&D intensity, ESG reputation, cost discipline) and short-term tactical moves (marketing adjustments), and translated findings into actionable playbooks for future rounds and policy-style recommendations.

Key Contributions

  • Built a decision analytics pipeline for 10-round simulation data (11 firms), including data reshaping, encoding, and multi-market/product integration to enable cross-round benchmarking.
  • Quantified drivers of long-term performance (CTSR) via ANOVA decomposition and a decision-tree-based importance framework, highlighting ESG reputation, production cost control, R&D intensity, and adjusted HR efficiency as stable contributors.
  • Separated long-term strategy vs. short-term tactics by contrasting static KPIs with dynamic “change-rate” KPIs; showed that short-term marketing adjustments can create temporary gains, while long-horizon strategic discipline dominates overall CTSR.
  • Segmented strategic archetypes using PCA + K-means to map firms into interpretable clusters (e.g., traditional outsourcing-focused vs. EV-focused innovators), enabling clearer competitive positioning.
  • Diagnosed supply–demand gaps with XGBoost classification and SHAP-style interpretation, finding production capacity as the key bottleneck while advertising/ESG can amplify demand pressure.
  • Modeled learning-curve advantage in EV strategy using LSTM time-series forecasting to illustrate how early EV scale-up can reduce unit costs over time and improve projected performance trajectory.

Toolkit & Methods

  • Pearson Correlation
  • PCA
